1. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how CardioAI ("CardioAI," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects information when you use the CardioAILive iOS application (the "App") and related backend services (together, the "Service"). This policy applies to patients and to clinical staff (nurses, cardiologists, and administrators) who use the App on behalf of a hospital or care organization.

By using the App, you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Patients: your name and email address, obtained through Sign in with Apple. If you choose to share your email through Apple's private relay, we receive and use the relay address you provide.
  • Clinical staff: your name, hospital email address, organization name, and role (nurse, cardiologist, or administrator), provided at signup. Clinical staff accounts require administrator approval before activation.

2.2 Health and Vitals Data

The core purpose of the App is to collect and relay cardiac and vitals data for remote patient monitoring. Depending on which features you use, this may include:

SourceData collected
Bluetooth (BLE) medical devices you pairHeart rate, ECG waveform data, blood pressure readings, blood oxygen saturation (SpO2), depending on the device type
Apple Health / HealthKitHeart rate data written to Apple Health by your Apple Watch or other HealthKit-connected sources, read with your explicit permission
Clinician-registered devicesIf a clinician registers a bedside monitoring device on your behalf (for example, if you are unable to pair a device yourself), the same categories of vitals data apply

We also generate derived clinical data from the above: AI-detected patterns (such as possible arrhythmia indicators), alert records, and clinical summary reports.

Fitness data (steps, active energy, workouts). If you use the App's Fitness tab, we also read step count, active energy burned, and workout data from Apple Health, with your explicit permission. This is meaningfully different from the vitals data described above: fitness data is used only to display your own activity trends within the App. It is not sent to our servers, not processed by the clinical AI pipeline, and not shared with your care team. It stays on your device and in your own Apple Health store, the same way it would for any other fitness app you use.

2.3 Device and Technical Information

We collect device identifiers for paired Bluetooth devices, app version information, and connection/session logs needed to operate the Service securely (for example, authentication tokens and audit logs of sign-ins and data-access events).

2.4 Subscription and Payment Information

CardioAI Live Premium is offered as an auto-renewing subscription through Apple's In-App Purchase system. We do not receive or store your payment card details. Apple processes all payments and shares with us only the information necessary to verify your subscription status (such as your subscription's product identifier and renewal status).

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To operate the core Service: processing your vitals data through our clinical AI pipeline to detect patterns and generate alerts
  • To connect you with your care team: routing alerts and reports to the clinical staff responsible for your care
  • To maintain your account and process subscription billing
  • To maintain the security and integrity of the Service, including detecting and preventing fraud or unauthorized access
  • To communicate with you about your account, service updates, or (for clinical staff) administrative matters such as account approval
  • To comply with legal obligations, including healthcare record-keeping requirements described in Section 6
We do not use your health data for advertising, and we do not sell your health data. This is a firm commitment, not just a policy choice — it is also a requirement of Apple's HealthKit terms for any app that reads Apple Health data.

4. How We Share Your Information

We share information only in the following circumstances:

  • With your care team: if you are receiving care from a hospital or clinical organization that has configured CardioAILive, your vitals data, alerts, and clinical reports are shared with the clinical staff at that organization. This is the central purpose of the Service.
  • With your hospital's electronic health record (EHR) system: for organizations that have configured this integration, clinically significant alerts may be written into your hospital's EHR system using healthcare data standards (FHIR R4) or sent via HL7 v2 messaging, so your care team's existing clinical systems reflect your monitoring data.
  • With service providers who help us operate the Service: including our cloud hosting provider and Apple (for authentication and subscription processing). These providers are only permitted to use your information to provide services to us.
  • For legal reasons: if required to do so by law, or in a good-faith belief that such action is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, protect the safety of any person, or investigate fraud or security issues.
  • With your consent: in any other circumstances where you have explicitly agreed to the sharing.

We do not sell your personal information or health data to third parties, and we do not share it with advertisers.

5. Apple Health, HealthKit & Third-Party Health Data

If you choose to connect Apple Health, the App requests permission to read heart rate data written by other apps or devices (such as Apple Watch), and, if you use the Fitness tab, step count, active energy burned, and workout data as well. You control each of these permissions individually through iOS Settings and can revoke any of them at any time. In accordance with Apple's requirements:

  • Heart rate data obtained through HealthKit is used to support your remote monitoring within the Service; step count, active energy, and workout data are used only to display your own activity trends in the Fitness tab. Neither is used for advertising or marketing purposes.
  • Health data obtained through HealthKit is not shared with third-party advertising networks or data brokers.

6. Data Retention

We retain your account and health information for as long as your account is active, and afterward as described below.

6.1 If you delete your account

You can delete your account at any time from within the App (Settings → Subscription & Account → Delete Account). When you do:

  • Your login credentials, name, and profile information are permanently deleted or anonymized, and you will not be able to sign in again.
  • Your vitals history, clinical alerts, and reports are retained as part of the medical record associated with your care, consistent with healthcare record-keeping requirements. In most U.S. jurisdictions, clinical records must be retained for a period of years regardless of a patient's request to delete their account — this is a feature of healthcare regulation generally, not a CardioAI–specific policy, and we do not have unilateral authority to erase records that a hospital's own record-keeping obligations require it to retain.

If you have questions about the retention of records held by your specific care organization, please contact that organization directly, as they are typically the ultimate custodian of your clinical record.

7. Your Rights and Choices

  • Access: you can view your own vitals, alerts, and reports at any time within the App.
  • Correction: if you believe information in your account (such as your name or email) is inaccurate, contact us using the information in Section 13.
  • Deletion: see Section 6 above.
  • Device disconnection: you can disconnect any paired BLE device or Apple Health connection at any time from the App's Connect tab.
  • Subscription management: manage or cancel your subscription at any time through Settings → Subscription & Account, which opens Apple's official subscription management screen.

8. Data Security

We use industry-standard safeguards to protect your information, including:

  • Encrypted transmission of data between the App and our servers
  • Secure on-device storage of authentication tokens and credentials (Apple's Keychain)
  • Role-based access control ensuring clinical staff can only access data for patients under their organization's care
  • HMAC-signed and authenticated connections for real-time device data streaming
  • Audit logging of account access and administrative actions

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent. If the App is used to monitor a minor's health, it must be done under the account and supervision of a parent, legal guardian, or the minor's treating clinical care team, consistent with applicable law. [This section should be reviewed by counsel if the Service is intended to support pediatric patients as a distinct use case.]

10. California and Other State Privacy Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights regarding your personal information under laws such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) or state-specific health data privacy laws (for example, Washington's My Health My Data Act, which regulates "consumer health data" collected outside of HIPAA's scope). These may include rights to know what information we collect, to request deletion, and to opt out of certain data uses.

11. A Note on HIPAA

CardioAI is designed with safeguards appropriate to sensitive health information, including encryption, access controls, and audit logging, as described in Section 8. However, whether HIPAA (the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) directly applies to CardioAI's handling of your data — and whether CardioAI acts as a HIPAA "business associate" of a given hospital — depends on the specific legal and contractual relationship between CardioAI and each care organization using the Service.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will notify you through the App or by other reasonable means before the changes take effect. Your continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
